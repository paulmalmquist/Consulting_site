Human-in-the-loop (HITL) is often framed as a temporary safeguard. In reality, it is a product requirement for any AI system that touches real decisions.

Executives care about accountability. When an AI system produces a recommendation, someone must own the outcome. HITL provides that ownership by defining who reviews, who approves, and what evidence is required.

The best HITL designs are explicit: they define thresholds, escalations, and response times. They are instrumented so the organization can see when humans intervene and why.
